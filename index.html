Kundan Mishra

PhD Candidate | Bioengineering | Electrochemical Biosensors | EIS | Agentic AI
I am a PhD Candidate in Bioengineering at The University of Texas at Dallas working on electrochemical biosensors, electrochemical impedance spectroscopy (EIS), assay development, microfluidic lab-on-a-chip systems, and translational sensing platforms for food, environmental, and health applications. My work spans sensor design, screen printing, functionalization, assay optimization, electrochemical testing, and validation in complex real-world matrices.

Highlights

  • PhD in Bioengineering at The University of Texas at Dallas with 4.0/4.0 GPA.
  • Developed intelligent multiplexed electrochemical impedance spectroscopy (EIS) biosensors for food and environmental sensing.
  • Validated sensing platforms in complex matrices including chicken, shrimp, milk, salad, water, and soil runoff.
  • Published work in Biosensors, Biosensors and Bioelectronics, Food Analytical Methods, Microchimica Acta, and related venues.
  • Awards include the ECS Jonsson Family Bioengineering Graduate Fellowship (2024–2025), Convergence Grant 2025, ECS Fall 2025 Travel Award, and BMES 2025 Travel Grant.

Selected Research

Food Safety

Multiplexed EIS Biosensors

Built and validated biosensor platforms for simultaneous detection of antibiotics, pesticides, pathogens, and toxins in real sample matrices.

Microfluidics

Lab-on-a-Chip Nanobiosensors

Contributed to microfluidic lab-on-a-chip nanobiosensor development and non-silicon nanofabrication research during the NNetRA program.

Diagnostics

Breath Biomarker Sensing

Worked on nanomaterial-enabled sensing systems for biomarker analysis, including molecular diabetic biomarkers in breath and MoS2-based electrochemical concepts.

Translation

Portable Sensor Platforms

Focused on sensor fabrication, assay optimization, portable electroanalytical systems, and real-world validation for translational deployment.
