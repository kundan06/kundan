Selected Projects

My projects focus on electrochemical biosensors, multiplexed impedance sensing, nano-enabled assay design, and translational sensing platforms for food safety, water quality, and clinical diagnostics. This page highlights representative systems spanning sensor fabrication, portable device development, microfluidics, and biomarker detection. Each project summary is based on my resume and research portfolio.

Intelligent Multiplexed EIS Biosensor for Food Safety

Developed a multiplexed electrochemical impedance spectroscopy platform for simultaneous detection of antibiotics and pesticides in complex food matrices.

EIS Multiplexed Sensing Food Safety Screen-Printed Sensors
  • Designed and fabricated sensors using screen-printing methods and full electrochemical workflows.
  • Validated performance in chicken, shrimp, milk, salad, and agricultural soil run-off matrices.
  • Performed spike-and-recovery, reproducibility, and matrix-effect testing for real-world translation.
UT Dallas Bioengineering
